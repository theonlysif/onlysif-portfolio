founder's office & growth at riffle.studio

i started as an (amateur) graphic design kid who loved writing at a marketing agency when i was 18 — first hire, no clue what i was doing.

one dangerous habit of looking at problems that weren't mine and going "i'll figure it out" became a career over time. rallied a lil' team after the agency (working with 155+ lovely clients), then working with founders in whom i saw an incredible passion, then employee #1 at places building from scratch — building community and running events at people+ai, then ai and some financial inclusion research at sthaan (where tanuj and a small team of us were exploring some initial cool product ideas and eventually consulting).

the common thread between what looks like seven years of doing a bit of everything was simple. i enjoy being the person who says "i'll figure it out" for people who carry a semblance of the passion i'd hope to build with, eventually. i like bringing out the best in people and doing good by them; soulless work does nothing for me. in retrospect, running my own team, what i was really doing was using it as a conduit to feel good about myself by uplifting others as much as i could. learned a lot of hard lessons doing that. still learning them. (whoops)

now i'm in the founder's office at riffle, building the figma for music with anurag, deo and a beautiful crew — and for the first time, i'm not the first hire. funny how that became a pattern :)

off the clock

i collect hyperlinks for fear of losing them, and annoy people by only sending the readwise version i've highlighted even if you want the original. i used to be insufferable about scheduling — slowly getting better.

i nerd out about planes and can't stop noticing how much i write about the passage of time (this year's birthday piece was going to be called "the march of time." my birthday's in march). i'm particular about where i go; if you know me, you already know where to find me.

eventually i want to build something of my own that solves a real problem. then something i care deeply about. then help others do the same.

i just like making things that help other people make things — let a man have fun :)
